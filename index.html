Primary Method

Rotary Brush
Encapsulation

Traditional steam cleaning floods carpets with water and takes 24–48 hours to dry — risking shrinkage, wicking stains, and mold. Our approach is scientifically different.

01

Pre-Inspection

We evaluate fiber type, soiling level, and stain locations — selecting the right encapsulation formula before a drop touches your carpet.

02

Low-Moisture Application

Crystalline polymer solution is applied with precision — wetting fiber tips only, never saturating backing or subfloor.

03

Rotary Brush Agitation Primary

Counter-rotating cylindrical brush drives deep into fiber — mechanically breaking the bond between embedded soil and carpet. Far deeper than any flat-surface machine.

04

Crystallize, Encapsulate & Extract

Soil is locked inside polymer crystals and vacuumed away completely. Carpets dry in 1–2 hours. No residue. No re-soiling. Done.

vs

Steam vs. Encapsulation

Steam uses gallons of water and takes 24–48 hours to dry. Encapsulation uses very low moisture — fiber tips only, never soaking through — and is dry in 1–2 hours with no mold, wicking, or shrinkage risk.

What is rotary brush encapsulation?
+
A counter-rotating cylindrical brush agitates deep into carpet fiber while a crystalline polymer solution surrounds each soil particle. Those crystals are vacuumed away completely — leaving carpets clean and dry in 1–2 hours with no residue.
Why does it dry so much faster than steam?
+
Physics. Steam cleaning soaks your carpet through to the backing with gallons of water — which takes 24–48 hours to evaporate. Our very low moisture (VLM) process only wets fiber tips, so there is far less water to evaporate. Most carpets are fully dry within an hour or two of our crew leaving.
Are there any hidden fees or travel charges?
+
None. The price you see is the price you pay — always. We serve Wichita, Derby, Andover, Goddard, and Maize with no added travel fees whatsoever.
How long does the appointment take?
+
Most cleanings take 1.5 to 2 hours depending on size and services. We work efficiently and thoroughly.
Do I need to be home?
+
No — but we need access. Many clients provide a garage code or arrange access through a neighbor. We'll keep you updated throughout.
Do you move furniture?
+
We move small items like couches and loveseats. For large sectionals, beds, or heavy furniture, please move those before we arrive.
Best package for pet stains and odor?
+
Pet Pro ($179) or Ultimate Clean ($219) — both include enzyme treatment and deodorizing. The rotary brush drives the enzyme solution deep into the fiber base where odor actually lives.
What payment do you accept?
+
Cash or credit card at time of service. We can also text a secure payment link for convenience.
